About Me

I’m a driven, adaptable Computer Science student moving into tech after 8+ years in sales, customer service, and intercultural work (Philippines, UAE, Denmark, Norway). Those roles have sharpened my problem-solving, teamwork, and communication—skills I now bring to software development.

I study Danish at AOF and FVU (limited professional proficiency) and work part-time at Scandic Hotel Aarhus City, with flexible hours that let me balance study and work effectively.

I like rapid prototyping—trying ideas fast and learning by building. With an AI assistant as my sounding board and typing helper, I iterate deliberately and prioritize safety. I often use LLMs to generate first-draft code, then I review, test, and refactor it so I fully understand what I ship. As a student still building fluency, I find this workflow makes it easier to try, learn, and build responsibly. I take a static-first approach for privacy and simplicity, and I’ve also built a dynamic Sales Inventory app (React + Express + PostgreSQL). I paused its Render deployment to harden the backend (authentication, input validation, rate-limiting, tests) and improve the deployment pipeline; it currently runs locally while I refine it, and I’ll redeploy with a safety-first setup.

Recently, I worked as a data analyst intern at Johannes Gutenberg University Mainz, where I applied machine learning to UK Biobank data on PM2.5 exposure and blood biomarkers. I’m excited to share our forthcoming article from this research.

Jeg læser dansk på AOF og FVU (begrænset professionelt niveau) og arbejder deltid på Scandic Hotel Aarhus City med fleksible timer, som gør det muligt at balancere studie og arbejde.

Jeg arbejder med hurtig prototyping—afprøver idéer hurtigt og lærer ved at bygge. Med en AI-assistent som sparringspartner og “skrivemakker” itererer jeg bevidst og prioriterer sikkerhed. Jeg bruger ofte LLM’er til første udkast af kode og gennemgår, tester og refaktorerer derefter, så jeg forstår det, jeg leverer. Som studerende, der stadig bygger flydende færdigheder, gør denne arbejdsgang det lettere at prøve, lære og bygge ansvarligt. Jeg har en static-first tilgang for privatliv og enkelhed og har også bygget en dynamisk Sales Inventory-app (React + Express + PostgreSQL). Jeg satte dens Render-deploy på pause for at styrke backenden (autentificering, inputvalidering, rate-limitering, tests) og forbedre deployments; den kører lokalt, mens jeg finpudser den, og bliver redeployet med en safety-first opsætning.
